How it works

three steps. one paste.

no timeline to scrub, no keyframes to set. paste a url, pick a look, ship the video.

01

paste.

drop your landing url. pyrocut opens the page, reads the brand — palette, type, logo, the hero copy — and pulls your real screens. no setup, no upload dance.

02

direct.

add a screenshot or a line of brief if you want, then pick a cut — dolly, snapcut, editorial — and a format. pyrocut storyboards the beats and times every move.

03

ship.

render in ~30s. download 1080p or 4k mp4 in 16:9, 9:16 or 1:1, or grab a share link → paste it straight into the launch post you already had open.

What pyrocut does

videos that look directed,
from a url you paste.

reads your whole brand.

palette, fonts, logo, even your hero copy — pulled straight from the url. the video comes out looking like your product, not a template.

moves like it was shot.

auto-zoom glides in on the moments that matter and a focus halo guides the eye. an ember ring pings on each beat — directed camera work, generated.

your brand on the backdrop.

three cuts, never the same.

every format, one render.

Questions

The fine print.

what do you actually need from me?
just a public url. pyrocut opens your landing, reads the brand and pulls real screens on its own. screenshots and a one-line brief are optional — they only make the cut sharper.
will it actually match my brand?
yes. it extracts your palette, fonts and logo from the page and carries them across the whole video — backdrop, captions, accents. you can edit any swatch before it renders if the auto-pick is off.
how long does a video take?
about thirty seconds from paste to playable. longer landings or 4k renders take a touch more, but you're never waiting on a render farm — progress shows on the scrubber, not a spinner.
what do i get to download?
1080p and 4k mp4 in 16:9, 9:16 and 1:1, plus a gif fallback and a caption track. download the file or hand someone a private share link.
can i use my own screenshots?
yes. drop in screens or a short brief and pyrocut works them into the storyboard instead of — or alongside — the ones it pulls from your url.
is my site data safe?
pyrocut only reads what's publicly on your landing page. nothing is kept beyond the screens it needs to render, and you can delete a project any time.
